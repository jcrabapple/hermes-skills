# Historical Research Report Formatting Guide

## Structure Template for Ancient Civilization Research Reports

### 1. Executive Summary (150-200 words)
- Hook: Most surprising or consequential finding
- Thesis statement
- Overview of key dimensions covered
- Why this matters now

### 2. Introduction (200-300 words)
- Historical context and timeline
- Scope definition
- Methodology (4-phase research approach)
- Sources overview

### 3. Core Sections by Dimension
Each major dimension gets its own section with:

**Format:**
```markdown
## [Dimension Name]

### [Subtopic 1]
- **Key Fact**: [Specific data point with source]
- **Example**: [Concrete real-world case]
- **Impact**: [How this influenced later civilizations]
- **Legacy**: [Modern manifestations]

### [Subtopic 2]
[Same structure]
```

**Recommended Dimensions for Ancient Civilizations:**
1. Political & Administrative Systems
2. Architectural Innovations
3. Writing & Record-Keeping
4. Scientific & Mathematical Contributions
5. Medical Knowledge & Practices
6. Agricultural & Environmental Adaptations
7. Trade & Economic Networks
8. Religious & Philosophical Influence
9. Linguistic Legacy
10. Military & Strategic Innovations
11. Cultural & Artistic Influence
12. Legal & Social Structures
13. Technological Innovations
14. Educational Systems
15. Global Reception & Modern Appropriation

### 4. Comparative Analysis (Optional but valuable)
- Comparison tables between Egyptian and contemporary civilizations
- Timeline of influence transmission
- Maps of trade/spread routes

### 5. Modern Legacy & Contemporary Relevance
- Current applications of ancient knowledge
- Ongoing archaeological discoveries
- Modern debates or reinterpretations
- Cultural appropriation vs. appreciation

### 6. Conclusion & Forward-Looking Implications
- Synthesis of key findings
- Unanswered questions
- Future research directions
- Why this civilization still matters

## Formatting Standards

### Headers
- Use ATX-style headers (#, ##, ###)
- Maximum depth: ### (3 levels)
- Consistent capitalization (Title Case for main sections, Sentence case for subsections)

### Citations
- Inline format: `*Source: [Author/Organization], "[Title]" ([Date])*`
- For web sources: Include URL
- For academic sources: Include author, title, publication, date
- Cite at end of each paragraph that contains sourced information

### Tables
- Use for comparisons, timelines, data summaries
- Keep columns minimal (3-4 max)
- Include clear headers
- Right-align numbers, left-align text

### Lists
- Use bullet points for non-sequential items
- Use numbered lists for processes/sequences
- Keep list items parallel in structure

### Bold & Italics
- **Bold**: Key terms, important concepts, section introductions
- *Italics*: Emphasis, foreign terms, book titles, quotes

## Voice & Style Guidelines

### Do:
- Use active voice
- Lead with concrete examples
- Use specific numbers and dates
- Attribute claims clearly
- Vary sentence length for readability
- Use subheadings liberally for scannability

### Don't:
- Start sections with background preamble
- Use passive voice excessively
- Make unsupported claims
- Over-explain obvious points
- Use travel-guide language ("boasts", "renowned")
- Include AI transitions ("Furthermore", "Moreover")

## Quality Checklist Before Delivery

- [ ] Every factual claim has a source citation
- [ ] Sections are organized by theme, not by source
- [ ] Information flows from most to least important
- [ ] Tables and visual elements are used where appropriate
- [ ] Voice is consistent and professional
- [ ] No banned patterns (see article-writing skill)
- [ ] Conclusion provides forward-looking value

## Tools & Queries for Historical Research

### Phase 1: Broad Exploration (8-10 searches)
- "[civilization] influence on modern world"
- "[civilization] contributions to [field]"
- "[civilization] vs [contemporary civilization]"
- "long term impact of [civilization]"
- "[civilization] legacy in [specific domain]"

### Phase 2: Deep Dive (per dimension)
- Architecture: "[civilization] building techniques influence on Greece Rome"
- Writing: "[civilization] writing system evolution into alphabet"
- Medicine: "[civilization] medical papyri influence on Greek medicine"
- Trade: "[civilization] trade routes economic networks"
- Religion: "[civilization] religious concepts influence on Judaism Christianity"

### Phase 3: Source Prioritization
1. Wikipedia (comprehensive overview)
2. World History Encyclopedia (authoritative context)
3. Academic journals (PubMed, NCBI, JSTOR)
4. National Geographic (archaeological insights)
5. University research centers
6. Museum publications

### Phase 4: Extract Strategy
- Extract 3-5 most authoritative sources per dimension
- Focus on sources with concrete data, examples, expert analysis
- Use browser_navigate for full content when web_extract times out
