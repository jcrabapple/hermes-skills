# GitLab Customer Success Engineer (CSE) Context

## Role Specifics

GitLab CSEs operate in a **pooled support model** — they are not account-dedicated. Instead, they support the **On-Demand Success Tier**, which means:
- Managing multiple concurrent customer engagements simultaneously
- No single-account ownership — customers rotate through the pooled team
- Need strong time management and prioritization skills
- Async-first collaboration across globally distributed team

## Key JD Requirements (Greenhouse 8498481002)

**Must-have:**
- Experience supporting 1+ GitLab use cases (SCM, CI/CD, DevSecOps, Agile Planning)
- Proficiency with DevSecOps or adjacent technical tooling
- Technical background in software development or systems engineering
- Strong communication skills: clear writing and live presentation
- Proven experience as trusted technical advisor in post-sales environment
- Strong time management/prioritization for pooled model
- Continuous learning mindset

**Culture Fit:**
- AI is a "core productivity multiplier" — all team members expected to integrate AI into workflows
- Values-driven, high-performance, collaborative
- Async collaboration model
- Continuous improvement mindset

## DevSecOps Domain Knowledge Needed

GitLab's platform covers:
- **Source Code Management (SCM)** — Git repositories, merge requests, code review
- **CI/CD** — Pipelines, runners, automation, deployment strategies
- **DevSecOps** — Security scanning, compliance automation, SAST/DAST
- **Agile Planning** — Issue boards, epics, milestones, roadmaps

Candidates should be able to discuss these concepts at an architectural level, even if they haven't used GitLab specifically.

## Resume Tailoring Strategy

**Summary lead:** "Customer Success Engineer with 10+ years delivering technical advisory and enablement for B2B SaaS platforms. Deep expertise in DevSecOps tooling, REST API integration, and cross-platform troubleshooting."

**Skills section order:**
1. DevSecOps & Platforms (GitLab, CI/CD, SCM, Agile, Docker)
2. Documentation & Enablement (technical writing, workshops, content strategy)
3. Identity & Access (SSO, SAML, OAuth)
4. API & Integration
5. Scripting & Automation
6. Observability & Data

**Experience bullets to emphasize:**
- Pooled/multi-customer engagement management
- Workshop and enablement content delivery
- Cross-functional GTM partnership (CSMs, AEs, Renewals)
- Adoption metrics and data-driven content prioritization
- Specialty competency development in platform technologies
- AI-augmented workflows

**De-emphasize:**
- Ticket volume and case resolution speed (signals "support IC")
- On-call metrics (less relevant for CSE)
- Pure troubleshooting without enablement component

## Interview Preparation

**Key questions to expect:**
1. "Tell us about a time you acted as a trusted technical advisor to an enterprise customer."
2. "How do you approach building enablement content for a technical audience?"
3. "How would you handle the pooled support model with multiple concurrent customer engagements?"
4. "Describe your experience with DevSecOps tooling."
5. "How do you stay current with platform developments and maintain specialty competency?"
