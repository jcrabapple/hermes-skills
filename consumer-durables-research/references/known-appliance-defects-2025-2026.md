# Known Appliance Defects and Failure Patterns (2025–2026)

> This is a living reference of documented widespread issues across major appliance brands.
> Documented = multiple independent owner reports OR expert/retailer confirmation.

## Front-Load Washers

### Electrolux (400/600/700 Series)
- **Dryer bearing failures** — grinding/metallic noise within months of purchase
  - Models affected: ELFE7437AT, ELFE7637AT, similar platform
  - Often requires 3–5 service visits; parts backordered
- **Out-of-box leaks** — split fill hose (P/N 5304526694 reported)
- **Excessive vibration** — described by techs as a "known issue at Electrolux"
- **Service network gaps** — Yale Appliance no longer sells the brand

### GE (GFW550 / GFW650 / GFW850 platform)
- **Control board failures** — inverter boards and main control boards failing
- **Drain pump shorts taking out the board** — cascade failure; pump shorts, fries control board
- **Door lock problems** — electronic door locks failing prematurely
- **Dryer drum cracking** — GFD55ESSNWW / GFD55ESSN2WW drums cracking at 18–24 months
  - Sharp edges inside drum after crack; replacement part WE21X29724
- **Vent pump issues** — UltraFresh vent fan running continuously or not running
- **Service rate**: 8.5% (GE Appliances), 10.5% (GE Profile) per Yale 2026

### Whirlpool (WFW5720 / WFW5620 platform)
- **Excessive vibration/shaking on spin** — "loud banging noise" reported by multiple owners
  - May be installation/leveleling-dependent but persistent enough to note
- **Non-user-accessible pump filter** — requires disassembling panels to clear blockages
- **Noise complaints** — described as "giant construction machine"
- **No catastrophic design defect** identified, but service rate middling

### LG (WM4000 / WM3400 / WM6700 platform)
- **OE drain error** — usually clogged pump filter or drain hose, not pump failure
  - Pump filter is user-accessible (bottom front panel)
- **Long-term drum spider corrosion** — aluminum spider corrodes after 5–10 years (hard water)
  - Not a defect; wear item on ALL front-loaders with this design
- **Service rate**: 2.7% (best in class per Yale 2026)

#### WM4000HWA / DLEX4000W Specifics
- **DLEX4000W sensor dry complaints** — recurring owner report that auto-sensing leaves clothes damp or wrinkled, requiring extra dry time. "Very Dry" or "More Dry" setting usually resolves. Cleaning sensor bars helps.
- **DLEX4000W aluminized drum** — step-up DLEX4500B has stainless steel drum; 4000W uses aluminized steel, which is more prone to discoloration over time.
- **DLEX4000W steam refresh underwhelming** — some owners report it does not effectively de-wrinkle.
- **WM3400CW budget alternative** — same 4.5 cu. ft. capacity, same cleaning performance, but no TurboWash (slower cycles), no steam, no WiFi. No additional defects vs. WM4000HWA; it's the same mechanical platform stripped of premium features.

## Spin Speed Reference

| RPM | Effect |
|-----|--------|
| 1,300 | Dryest clothes; industry standard for premium front-load |
| 1,160 | Noticeably wetter; longer dry times |
| 1,000 | Basic front-load (Whirlpool budget, some Samsung) |
| 750 | Top-load agitator machines |
