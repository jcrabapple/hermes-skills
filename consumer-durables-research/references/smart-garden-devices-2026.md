## Market Overview

Two categories: **bird feeder cameras** (seed feeders) and **bird bath/fountain cameras** (water features).

## Key Pattern: Hardware + Subscription AI Model

Most smart bird feeders use a "cheap hardware + recurring AI subscription" model:
- Hardware is $50-200, but AI bird identification is locked behind $3-5/mo or $45-70/yr.
- **Look for "lifetime AI" bundles** — Birdfy offers this on their direct site. It's always the better value.
- Without AI, you still get motion-triggered video, live view, notifications. You can use the free Cornell Lab Merlin Bird ID app as a workaround.
- Cloud storage is typically free with 30-day rolling retention. Local microSD is rare.

### Bird Bath Caveats
- Fountain pumps clog with algae/debris — WIRED reviewer's pump died after 2 seasons
- Shallow basins need refilling every 2-3 days in dry weather
- Birds may take weeks to discover and use a new bath
- Place rocks inside for birds to gauge depth (they avoid baths where they can't see the bottom)

## Source Priority for Smart Garden Devices

Unlike major appliances, reliability databases don't exist for these products. Use:
1. **WIRED** — Kat Merck tests bird feeders year-round, best expert source
2. **Wirecutter (NYT)** — covers smart bird feeders category
3. **Amazon reviews** — look for volume (1,000+ reviews) and 1-star patterns
4. **Reddit r/birdfeeding** — unfiltered owner experiences
5. **Manufacturer direct site** — for coupon codes, bundle options, lifetime AI availability

## Comparison Factors to Check

- Cloud storage free tier and retention period
- MicroSD card support (Birdfy v1 does NOT support it despite having a slot)
- Wi-Fi: most are 2.4 GHz only (no 5 GHz) — range matters for yard placement
- Solar panel: included vs optional vs separate mount
- Seed capacity: ranges from 1L (budget) to 1.5L (Birdfy v1) to 3.5L (dual-bin models)
- IP rating: IP65 minimum for outdoor use
- Family sharing: Birdfy allows 20+ guest accounts
